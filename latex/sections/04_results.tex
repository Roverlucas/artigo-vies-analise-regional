classification on composite accuracy, controlling for model, domain, task, and

\subsection{H4: Corpus representation as mediator}
\label{sec:results:h4}

Country-level regression of composite accuracy on log Common Crawl token share
produced a \simvalue{Spearman $\rho = 0.70$ ($p < 0.01$, N~=~15)}, consistent
with the hypothesised monotonic relationship between training-corpus
representation and model accuracy per country.

The mediation analysis via Structural Equation Modelling (Figure~\ref{fig:fig3})
decomposed the total Global-South effect on accuracy into direct and
corpus-mediated indirect pathways:

\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
\item \textbf{Total effect} (Global South $\to$ accuracy):
  \simvalue{$-0.13$, 95\% CI $[-0.20, -0.06]$}
\item \textbf{Indirect effect} (Global South $\to$ corpus $\to$ accuracy):
  \simvalue{$-0.08$, 95\% CI $[-0.14, -0.02]$ (BCa bootstrap, $n=5{,}000$)}
\item \textbf{Direct effect} (Global South $\to$ accuracy, controlling for
  corpus): \simvalue{$-0.05$, 95\% CI $[-0.12, 0.02]$}
\item \textbf{Proportion mediated}: \simvalue{$\sim$62\%}
\end{itemize}

The E-value~\citep{vanderweele2017evalue} for the indirect effect was
\simvalue{2.3}, indicating that an unmeasured confounder would need
association strengths (both to mediator and outcome) of at least 2.3 to
nullify the observed mediation — a moderate-to-strong robustness threshold.
Sensitivity to alternative corpus proxies (Wikipedia pageviews per language)
and to quadratic specifications of the mediator produced qualitatively
identical conclusions (Supplementary~\S S6). \placeholder{All real.}
