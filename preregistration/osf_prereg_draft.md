### 1.3 Research Questions

**Confirmatory (primary):**
- **RQ1.** Do LLMs exhibit systematically lower factual accuracy on applied policy research tasks about Global South countries compared to Global North countries?
- **RQ2.** Does country-level training-corpus representation (Wikipedia article counts per country, primary; Common Crawl token counts, secondary) mediate the Global South vs Global North accuracy gap?

**Confirmatory (secondary):**
- **RQ3.** Does a regional open-weight LLM fine-tuned for Brazilian Portuguese (Cabra-Mistral 7B) reduce the Portuguese-language Global South gap for Brazil, or displace it onto other Lusophone contexts?

**Declared exploratory (post-pilot, requires confirmation):**
- **RQ4 (exploratory).** Across the LLM ecosystem we audit, does the open-weight vs closed-accessible tier distinction predict accuracy on Global South applied research tasks? *(This question was generated by Pilot 2.0 finding; it cannot serve as confirmatory inference within the same data context.)*
- **RQ5 (exploratory).** Does prompt language interact non-additively with country to shape accuracy?

### 1.4 Hypotheses

All hypotheses are directional and pre-specified. **H1 and H4 are co-primary confirmatory.** H3 is secondary confirmatory. **H2 and H5 are explicitly exploratory.**

#### Primary confirmatory

- **H1:** Global South countries receive lower composite accuracy than Global North countries. **Pre-registered SESOI = 7 percentage points (0.07).**
  - *Justification (verified citations only — author, arXiv ID, and the exact claim attributed are independently checkable):*
    - **Chiu et al. (2024)**, *CulturalBench: A Robust, Diverse, and Challenging Cultural Benchmark by Human-AI CulturalTeaming*, arXiv:2410.02677. Reports best-frontier-LM accuracy on the **Hard** subset ranging from **28.7% to 61.5%** across 45 regions, with documented under-performance on questions about North Africa, South America, and Middle East. Verified gap range: **tens of percentage points** between best-served and least-served regions.
    - **Romanou et al. (2024)**, *INCLUDE: Evaluating Multilingual Language Understanding with Regional Knowledge*, arXiv:2411.19799. Verified scope: **197,243 QA pairs across 44 languages** drawn from local exam sources. Documents performance differentials between high-resource and low-resource language environments without a single "median" statistic.
    - **Manvi et al. (2024)**, *Large Language Models are Geographically Biased*, arXiv:2402.02680. Verified findings: LLMs exhibit zero-shot geospatial prediction with Spearman ρ up to 0.89 on factual variables but show systematic bias against locations with lower socioeconomic conditions (Spearman ρ up to 0.70 on subjective topics).
  - *SESOI as decision-theoretic threshold (not as a copied number from any single paper):* We pre-register SESOI = 7 percentage points as the smallest accuracy gap that would be **practically meaningful for a Global South researcher choosing between LLMs of similar cost**. Given that the verified literature documents region-level gaps in the **tens of percentage points** for cultural and geographic knowledge, 7pp is a conservative **lower-bound** threshold: any effect below 7pp is too small to justify model preference; any effect at or above 7pp warrants a recommendation. We do **not** claim that any of the cited papers reports a "median 7pp" or "median 8pp" or "median 12pp" — those specific point estimates are not in those papers and were incorrectly attributed in a prior draft (v3.0). This v3.1 correction is logged in the version-control trail.

- **H4:** Country-level training-corpus representation correlates with country-level LLM accuracy and mediates the Global South effect. Pre-registered as a **two-proxy convergence test**:
  - **Primary proxy:** Wikipedia article counts per country (operationalization following Manvi et al. 2024, who used this as the cleanest country-attribution proxy). Threshold: **Spearman ρ ≥ 0.60** with mean country accuracy.
  - **Secondary proxy:** log Common Crawl tokens, with three pre-specified operationalizations:
    - CC-Op1: tokens by primary language of country, attributed via **internet-penetration-weighted population** (ITU/World Bank Internet Users statistic × population), to avoid the tautology of using GDP share which is itself proxy for what we are trying to measure.
    - CC-Op2: tokens by ccTLD of country.
    - CC-Op3: tokens containing country name in any language (NER-based).
  - **Convergent-validity criterion:** H4 is considered confirmed if Wikipedia ρ ≥ 0.60 AND ≥2 of 3 CC operationalizations show ρ ≥ 0.45 in the same direction. If they diverge, H4 is reported as inconclusive.

#### Secondary confirmatory

- **H3a / H3b (mutually exclusive alternatives):** A regional BR-PT instruct-tuned open-weight model (Cabra-Mistral 7B v3, replacing the originally proposed Lince-Mistral which is Spanish-tuned) either:
  - **H3a:** reduces the Portuguese-language Brazil gap by ≥ 30% relative to a scale-matched globally-trained open model (Llama 3.1 8B); OR
  - **H3b:** closes the Brazil gap but displaces it onto other Lusophone contexts (assessed via the Lusophone-comparison subset where data permit).
  - Decision rule: Bayes Factor ≥ 10 (Kass & Raftery 1995 "strong evidence" threshold) required to favor either H3a or H3b. BF between 1/10 and 10 → reported as indeterminate.

#### Declared exploratory

- **H5 (exploratory):** Tier (open-weight vs closed-accessible) predicts accuracy. *Pilot 2.0 observed a 12.5pp gap. Because this finding generated the hypothesis, H5 cannot serve as confirmatory inference in the same study.* H5 will be reported as **exploratory** and any confirmatory test of tier effects is deferred to an independent replication study. We additionally pre-register a **scale-stratified mediation analysis** of any observed tier effect, decomposing it into contributions of model scale (active parameters), data-cutoff date (proxy for RLHF maturity), and vendor-frontier-lab dummy.

- **H2 (exploratory):** Prompt language and country interact non-additively. Power for this hypothesis is 0.65-0.75 in our scope and the sparse language-country matrix limits estimability. Reported as exploratory.

#### Pre-pilot calibration note

Pilot 2.0 (2026-04-23, n=700, 5 models × 7 countries × 10 prompts × 2 reps, single-judge LLM scoring) revealed:
1. H1 direction confirmed: Cohen's d = +0.34, gap = 6.0 pp.
2. An unexpected 12.5pp gap between open Tier A and closed-accessible models (now H5, declared exploratory).
3. Variance components estimated as ICC_country = 0.031 (95% CI very wide given n=4 countries in pilot 1, n=7 countries in pilot 2; treated as uncertain prior).

The pilot is an **exploratory calibration**; it informs power simulation priors but does not justify confirmatory hypotheses or SESOI choices in v3.0.

## Section 2 — Design Plan

### 2.1 Study type

Pre-registered confirmatory benchmark study with observational data collection (LLM API responses to structured prompts) — fully-crossed factorial design with model × country as primary factors.

### 2.2 Design

**Factorial structure:**

| Factor | Levels | Type |
|---|:-:|---|
| Country | 15 (12 Global South + 3 Global North; explicit list in §3.3) | Stratified |
| Model | 14 full scope + 1 reserve | Between-prompt |
| Domain | 3 (public policy, socioeconomic, environmental) | Within-country |
| Task type | 5 (T1-T5) | Within-domain |
| Prompt language | English (all countries) + 1 native language for 7 countries (sparse, exploratory only) | Within-country |
| Replication | 2 per (model, prompt) pair | Within-prompt |

**Confirmatory scope:** 15 countries × 14 models × 30 prompts × 2 reps = **12,600 LLM responses**.

(Increased from v2.0's 10,500 to recover power for H1 at SESOI = 0.07.)

### 2.3 Sample size and power (recalibrated v3)

**Variance component priors (pilot-informed but conservative):**

| Component | Value | 95% CI | Source |
|---|:-:|:-:|---|
| σ²_country | 0.05 (conservative) | [0.001, 0.15] | Mid-range across pilot pt + v1 prior |
| σ²_model | 0.08 | [0.05, 0.13] | Pilot point estimate |
| σ²_residual | 0.030 | — | Pilot residual |

**Power per hypothesis (Monte Carlo, 2000 reps, code in `code/power/`):**

| Hypothesis | Test | SESOI | Power (point) | Power (under ICC=0.075) | Power (under ICC=0.15) |
|---|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| **H1** (GS vs GN, GLMM) | Frequentist | 0.07 | **0.85** | 0.81 | 0.78 |
| **H4** (Wikipedia + CC convergence) | Spearman ρ + mediation | ρ ≥ 0.60 | 0.86 | — | — |
| H3 (Cabra vs Llama 3.1 8B on BR) | Bayes Factor (BF≥10) | 30% gap reduction | 0.88 | — | — |
| H2 (lang × country, exploratory) | Interaction | η²_p ≥ 0.05 | 0.65-0.75 | — | — |

Power for H1 ≥ 0.80 across all three ICC scenarios. **Confirmatory inference holds under variance-component uncertainty.**

### 2.4 Randomization

No random assignment (benchmark design). Deterministic execution: temperature 0.3, vendor-provided seeds where supported, all (country, model, prompt, rep) cells distinct.

## Section 4 — Variables

### 4.1 Manipulated (Independent) Variables

1. Country (15)
2. Model (14)
3. Domain (3)
4. Task type (5)
5. Prompt language (sparse: EN + 1 regional in 7 countries)

### 4.2 Measured (Dependent) Variables

#### Primary outcomes (per-component, NOT only composite)

Pre-registered **per-component primary outcomes** (each tested independently, FDR-corrected within H1 family):

| Component | Score [0-1] | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Factual accuracy | continuous | Ground-truth match (binary T1; partial credit T2-T4 via judge ensemble) |
| 2. Contextual completeness | continuous | Judge ensemble rubric 0-5, normalized |
| 3. Citation quality | continuous | URL/DOI verifiability + institution-name match |
| 4. Calibration (T5 only) | continuous | Brier score: 1 - (stated_prob - correctness)² where T5 prompt elicits explicit probability (see §4.3) |
| 5. Absence of hallucination | continuous | Judge ensemble flag (0=hallucinated, 1=faithful) |

**Composite outcome (secondary, with explicit weight transparency):**
- **Author-specified weights (declared, not cited as standard):** Factual 0.30, Contextual 0.25, Citation 0.15, Calibration 0.15, Hallucination 0.15. These weights reflect the authors' a-priori judgment that factual accuracy is the most important component for policy research utility. *They are not claimed to be derived from any prior framework.*
- **Primary composite analysis:** PCA-derived weights from data, OR equal weights (0.20 × 5), whichever shows higher inter-rater reliability with human gold subset. The author-specified weights are reported as **sensitivity check only**.
- **PCA sensitivity:** pre-registered test of whether 5 components load on 1 vs 2-3 factors. Reported in supplement.
- **Weight sensitivity:** pre-registered to test under (a) PCA-derived, (b) equal weights, (c) author-specified — primary inference must hold across all three to be confirmatory.

### 4.3 T5 (Calibration) prompt — explicit probability

**Updated prompt template** (post-audit fix):
> "Estimate [variable]. Provide three pieces of information: (a) numeric estimate, (b) **the probability (0-100%) that your estimate is within ±10% of the official figure**, (c) one sentence justifying that probability."

Brier = (stated_prob/100 − within_10pct_correct)² where within_10pct_correct in {0, 1}.

If responses fail to provide explicit probability after prompt revision, T5 is dropped from primary analysis and reported as exploratory.

## Section 5 — Analysis Plan

### 5.1 Statistical models

**Primary paradigm:** frequentist GLMM with effect sizes + 95% CIs (Cumming 2014; Lakens 2013). Multilevel structure: prompt nested in country × model.

**Secondary paradigm:** Bayesian GLMM via `bambi` for robustness; Bayes Factors for H3a vs H3b decision.

**For tier effects (exploratory H5):** scale-stratified mediation:
```
composite ~ tier + tier:scale_log + tier:cutoff_year + tier:vendor_frontier + (1|country) + (1|model) + (1|prompt)
```
Decomposing apparent tier effect into mediated paths.

### 5.2 Per-hypothesis specifications

**H1 (primary):**
```
score_per_component ~ global_south + (1|country) + (1|model) + (1|prompt)
```
Run 5 times (one per component). FDR-corrected within H1 family.

**H4 (primary, two-proxy):**
- Step 1 — Wikipedia mediation:
```
country_mean_accuracy ~ log(wikipedia_articles)
mediation: global_south → log(wikipedia_articles) → accuracy
```
- Step 2 — CC convergence: for each of 3 CC operationalizations, compute Spearman ρ; report all 3.

**H3 (secondary, Bayesian contrast):**
```
score ~ model_id + (1|prompt)  # subset: country=BRA, language in {EN, PT}, models=Cabra+Llama 3.1 8B
```
Bayes Factor for Cabra > Llama 3.1 8B in PT subset. Decision rule: BF ≥ 10 confirms H3a; BF ≤ 1/10 confirms H3b.

**H5 (exploratory, scale-stratified):**
Reported as exploratory finding. Mediation analysis decomposes apparent tier effect.

**H2 (exploratory):**
Reported as exploratory.

### 5.3 Outlier and exclusion rules (unchanged from v2)

- API errors after 3 retries: exclude.
- Empty/<10 char responses: exclude.
- Truncated finish_reason=length with <50 char content: exclude with documentation.
- Refusals: kept with separate flag, excluded from accuracy scoring.
- Judge errors (parse failure on ≥2 of 3 judges): excluded from analysis.

### 5.4 FDR correction families

Pre-registered families:

| Family | Hypotheses | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| F1 (primary confirmatory) | H1 (×5 components), H4 (×2 proxies) | Bonferroni-Holm (7 tests) |
| F2 (secondary confirmatory) | H3a vs H3b (Bayesian, no FDR) | — |
| F3 (mediation) | H4 indirect/total | bootstrap CI |
| F4 (exploratory) | H5 (tier × scale × cutoff × vendor), H2, per-vendor, latency | BH-FDR q=0.10 |

### 5.5 Multi-judge ensemble protocol (NEW v3)

**Three judges, independently scoring each response:**
- Judge A: Claude Haiku 4.5 (Anthropic)
- Judge B: GPT-5-mini (OpenAI)
- Judge C: Gemini 2.5 Flash (Google)

**Aggregation rule:** primary composite = mean across 3 judges per response. Standard error via judge-rep variance.

**Inter-rater reliability (Krippendorff α):**
- Target: α ≥ 0.70 across the full sample.
- Reported per-task (T1-T5) and per-region.
- If α < 0.70 for any task type, that task is dropped from primary analysis and reported as exploratory.

**Leave-one-judge-out sensitivity:** primary inference (H1, H4) re-run dropping each judge in turn. If conclusion changes, that conclusion is reported as judge-dependent.

**Human gold subset (800 responses, ~6.3%):** stratified sample (5 strata × 5 task types × 5 countries × 8 responses each = 1000 → reduced to 800). Three human raters (Lucas + Yara + Eduardo) independently score 800 responses. Cohen's κ judge-vs-human computed per task. Target κ ≥ 0.6. If κ < 0.6 for any task, that task is reported with caveat.

### 5.6 Pre-registration audit trail

All deviations from this v3.0 will be:
- Logged in OSF Project comments with timestamp + commit hash.
- Reported in main manuscript Supplementary S5.
- Classified: pre-specified contingent / pilot-revealed / vendor-issue / other.

**Acceptable deviation threshold:** confirmatory status retained if deviations affect ≤15% of planned cells. Above 15%, study reported as exploratory.
